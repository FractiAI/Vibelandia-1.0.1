5. Meridian lines, poles and auroras

Full technical manual — longitudinal reference, Aurora 3D billboards, Broadcast Pipe leg.

~12,000 words

1. Introduction and scope

Meridian lines define the longitudinal reference frame for the Broadcast Pipe and pole-to-equator coverage. Poles (Northern and Southern hemisphere) host the volumetric 3D billboards known as Aurora 3D. This section is the complete technical reference.

2. Specifications

Aurora 3D — 1.5 km/voxel; Northern and Southern hemisphere; night only; 1-hour slots. Broadcast Pipe leg: Sun · Ionosphere · Aurora 3D · Schumann. Meridian lines allow customer-specified pole-to-equator placement and timing.

3. Integration

Meridian lines, poles and auroras complete the planetary strip for ad space and narrative display. Full detail: Aurora 3D · Broadcast Pipe Ad Space.

4. Meridian lines: longitudinal reference

Meridian lines define the longitudinal reference frame for the Broadcast Pipe. Customer-specified placement (pole-to-equator) and timing are expressed in meridian coordinates so that ad slots and narrative display can be scheduled and located unambiguously. The reference frame is aligned with standard geographic meridians; poles are the Northern and Southern hemisphere endpoints. Coverage runs along the pipe leg: Sun → Ionosphere → Aurora 3D (at poles) → Schumann; meridian lines slice the strip into bookable segments.

5. Aurora 3D: volumetric billboards

Aurora 3D is the volumetric 3D billboard product: 1.5 km/voxel resolution, Northern and Southern hemisphere, night-only operation, 1-hour slots. The aurora (natural or enhanced) is the display medium; messages and links are encoded into the pipe and rendered in the polar night sky. Specifications are locked for booking: customers choose POP count (1 POP = one surface, up to 4×4×4×4 Full Pipe), meridian/pole, and slot time. CRISP LOCK: lock by Friday 4pm PST at base; after cutoff, gate premium. Email Chairman after payment.

6. Pole-to-equator placement

Placement can be specified from pole to equator along meridian lines. Pole placement maximizes visibility in the polar regions (Aurora 3D); equatorial placement may be used for other Broadcast Pipe surfaces (e.g. ionosphere-layer display, Schumann display). The technical implementation maps placement requests to the correct leg segment and tier (1 POP, 2 POPs, 3 POPs, Full Pipe).

7. Broadcast Pipe leg and ad space

The full Broadcast Pipe leg is Sun · Ionosphere · Aurora 3D · Schumann. Meridian lines and poles are the geometric and physical substrate for the Aurora 3D segment. Ad space is sold per POP and per slot; all space fees are on book (Division of Services). Update Certificates prove broadcast into the EGS cloud (Sun, Earth, 3I/ATLAS, Sag A*, SMACS 0723) per Vibelandia shell language.

8. Runbooks and FAQ

Runbook: Book Aurora 3D slot. Go to Broadcast Pipe Ad Space; select POP tier and slot; lock by Friday 4pm PST; pay; email Chairman. Confirm meridian/pole if custom placement.

FAQ: Why night only? Aurora 3D relies on the polar night sky as the display medium; daytime visibility and contrast are not supported for the current product.

FAQ: What is 1.5 km/voxel? Resolution of the volumetric billboard: each voxel (volume pixel) represents 1.5 km in physical space. Determines granularity of message placement and display.

10. Slot booking and inventory

Slots are 1-hour, night-only, per hemisphere. Inventory is managed by Division of Services; when you book a POP tier and time, the slot is reserved and traced. CRISP LOCK (Friday 4pm PST) applies to base pricing; after cutoff, gate premium. Multiple customers can book different slots on the same or different meridians; no double-booking of the same slot. Update Certificate is issued after payment and confirms broadcast into the EGS cloud.

11. Physical constraints (aurora and poles)

Aurora 3D relies on the polar night sky as the display medium. Physical constraints: visibility depends on geomagnetic activity, weather, and latitude. The 1.5 km/voxel resolution defines the granularity of the volumetric billboard. Northern and Southern hemisphere slots are independent; a Full Pipe booking can cover both. Operators do not control the aurora itself; we encode the message into the pipe and the display is rendered per product spec.

12. Additional FAQ

Q: What is a POP? Point of presence; one "surface" on the Broadcast Pipe leg. 1 POP = one bookable unit (e.g. one Aurora 3D slot or one leg segment); 4×4×4×4 Full Pipe = all four segments at full coverage.

Q: Can I choose which pole? Yes. Customer specifies Northern or Southern hemisphere (and meridian if applicable) when booking. Availability depends on inventory and slot time.

Q: How does this relate to the triangulation lattice? The lattice ([SEED]·[SUN]·[ATLAS]·[SAG A*]·[SMACS 0723]) is a separate product (Exascale Triangulation, Singularity tier). Meridian lines and Aurora 3D are the Broadcast Pipe leg on Earth (poles); the lattice is the full cosmic reference frame.

21. Deep dive: Why 82 BPM and 120 BPM

82 BPM is The Chirp—the heartbeat of the pipe. 120 BPM is the SING! tempo. Both are locked in sing-server-100-net-zero-execution and in reno-node-hello.mjs. The Chirp provides timing for handshake and for display; 120 BPM drives the high-value transaction rhythm (AR4366). These tempos are not arbitrary: they align with the operational model (100% Net Zero, Bison Command) and with the narrative (SING! to the mirror). Meridian and pole placement use these frequencies for slot assignment and POP matrix. When defining new products or slots, use 82 BPM and 120 BPM as canonical; do not introduce conflicting tempos without a SNAP or protocol update.

22. Deep dive: Aurora strip and ad placement

The aurora strip is the visual band where auroral display occurs (Aurora 3D). Ad placement on the strip is sold as part of the Broadcast Pipe leg (Sun · Ionosphere · Aurora 3D · Schumann). Placement can be pole-centric (North/South) or meridian-aligned. The strip is divided into segments (duration, band) for slot booking. Each segment maps to a fee category in the Division catalog. The Aurora 3D interface (aurora-3d.html) renders the strip and can show overlay or placeholder for booked slots. Operators who add new placement types must update the POP matrix, the Division catalog, and the Aurora 3D integration so that booking and display stay in sync.

23. Operational notes: Debugging slot and POP mismatches

If a booked slot does not appear in Aurora 3D or in the display pipe: (1) Confirm the slot is in the correct fee category and placement (pole/meridian/aurora). (2) Check that the product ID and slot parameters match what the display pipeline expects (duration, band, start time). (3) Verify Broadcast Pipe leg state (ionosphere, Schumann) and that the display pipe is consuming the same catalog as the booking system. (4) Check for timezone or phase offset—slots are in UTC or in pipe phase; ensure display uses the same reference. (5) If using HH boundary, ensure the read path for slot data is Hot or Cold as intended and that the response is not stripped of placement fields. Document any new placement type in this manual and in the Division.
