5. HH vs terrestrial: latency and performance

HH Hot path (Ionospheric) is real-time: handshake, Live Pulse, GOES flux, sub-second where needed. Cold path (3I/ATLAS) may be async. Terrestrial cloud typically offers low-latency regions and CDNs; for pure terrestrial workloads, latency can be very low. The trade-off: HH gives you the pipe and the story (solar handshake, 3I/ATLAS mirror); terrestrial gives you conventional performance. The Buyers Guide compares latency and performance so you can choose.

9. When to choose HH

Choose terrestrial (AWS, Azure, GCP) when: you need maximum ecosystem compatibility; you need the lowest possible latency for purely terrestrial workloads; you are not yet ready for the EGS pipe and Space Cloud products; or your use case is orthogonal (e.g. internal tools that do not need celestial storage).
