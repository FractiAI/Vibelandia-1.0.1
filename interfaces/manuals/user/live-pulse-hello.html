Monitor Live Pulse for 21.4 Hz handshake, Kp-index, and HELLO confirmation. HELLO renders when Kp-index ≥ 5 (G1 Storm) and the binary header SPACE_CLOUD.EGS.VIBELANDIA.RENO is successfully training the stream.

3. What Live Pulse shows

Live Pulse displays: 60 BPM heartbeat; 21.4 Hz overlay (3rd overtone Schumann carrier); 1.618 Hz Signature Chirp (Φ); GOES X-ray flux telemetry; Kp-index; handshake state; triangulation lattice ([SEED]·[SUN]·[ATLAS]·[SAG A*]·[SMACS 0723]); and, when conditions are met, the HELLO block. The page is a thin client: data comes from telemetry.json, handshake.log, and NOAA flux.

4. When HELLO appears

HELLO renders when (1) Kp-index ≥ 5 (G1 Storm) and (2) the binary header SPACE_CLOUD.EGS.VIBELANDIA.RENO is successfully training the stream (e.g. telemetry or stream state has space_cloud_stream.training === true). Both conditions must be met. HELLO is the confirmation block that the pipe is live and the stream is trained. It appears on the zero-state or mission-takeover area of Live Pulse; optionally on Space Cloud Division. If you don’t see HELLO, check Kp and stream state; the technical manual (Sun, Ionosphere) has runbooks.

Users don’t need to “do” anything with these; they are part of the display and the proof that the pipe is phase-locked.

19. Deep dive: What the heartbeat and overlay mean

The heartbeat is the timestamp of the last handshake cycle. It updates every 8 minutes when the solar-handshake workflow runs. When you see a fresh heartbeat, the pipe has just received a new push (handshake.log appended, telemetry.json updated). The 21.4 Hz overlay is the primary carrier (3rd overtone Schumann / NPM Hawaii) for the handshake and Morse payload. The 1.618 Hz overlay is the Signature Chirp (Golden Ratio Φ). Together they form the phase-lock that the Sun (AR4366) and 3I/ATLAS mirror use. You do not need to "read" these numbers for daily use; they indicate that the pipe is active and phase-locked. If the heartbeat is stale (e.g. more than 10 minutes old), the handshake workflow may be down; operators can check the workflow and repo.

20. Deep dive: GOES flux and physical receipt

GOES X-ray flux is displayed on Live Pulse (from NOAA). After a handshake push, we watch for an M-class or X-class spike from AR4366 within roughly 16 minutes. If we see such a spike, we treat it as physical confirmation that the Source (the Sun) has "received" our signal—i.e. the solar activity aligns with our handshake timing. This receipt is confirmatory, not mandatory: the handshake is valid even if no spike is observed. Receipt supports mission verification and narrative (e.g. "we got a spike"). Users can use Live Pulse to see the flux and, if they know the handshake timing, to check for receipt. The technical manual (Sun section) has the full runbook for handshake verification.

Kp is the planetary K-index (0–9); we gate HELLO on Kp ≥ 5 (G1 Storm). Kp varies with space weather and is not under our control. So if the sky is quiet (Kp < 5), HELLO will not show by design. If the pipe has not locked to the header, HELLO will not show. In either case, the pipe may still be active—check the heartbeat and CometMirror.
